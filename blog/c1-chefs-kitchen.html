Dev Bootcamp: Let's Do This

July 10, 2015

So as of today I have been in Dev Bootcamp for five days, and am almost done with week 1 of Phase 0. Woo hoo! How is it so far? Thrilling and lonely and exciting and frustrating and repetitive and joyful. I actually feel like something is being released from inside of me that I have probably been pushing down for quite a long time, a sort of validating of what I like to do.

I knew this coming in, and have previous experience with guided remote learning, but it still hit me: This is not going to be handed to me, I have to reach out and grab it. All of the answers are not given; that's what Google is for! And there is no one to sit down with face-to-face and talk to, not for another nine weeks. That is cool with me, but also a reminder: I have to do this. It sounds like, as time goes on and I get to know my cohort, this will become we have to do this.

Grit is an important part of life. It is clear the DBC experience aims to produce grit, almost like a dare: DBC is daring me to push through, to learn how to push and keep pushing. So far it is working. Definitely not a consumer culture, thank God. I won't learn anything if it is handed to me.

Admission: It is a new thing for me to engage the social networking scene. But I do agree it is vital to do so. I have avoided Twitter and rarely look at Facebook. That has changed today! And it needed to. DBC is pushing me into the social networking scene, almost like I am finally fully entering into world of the internet via the Twitterverse. I have avoided it to keep from wasting time but also to avoid potential criticism from others. There is a fear factor involved: what will people think of what I post? DBC is pushing me to find out, that is for sure.

After this past week my expectations for Phase 0 are more grounded: it is going to be hard work, grinding, pushing through, time management, time boxing, time, time time, work, work, work. Doing things I want to do and things I don't want to do. But my mind is opening and I am getting it. At least so far.

The journey has begun!
