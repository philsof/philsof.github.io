Time to Reflect: Week 3

Or: don't jump, life is worth living

July 26, 2015

Ok the subtitle is a joke, I just wanted to get your attention.

Time to reflect on Dev Bootcamp so far. Well, it's week 3, and I feel like I am getting it. "It" being the stuff I am supposed to be learning. Part of this may be my "abstract random" thinking style, which is freakin awesome. I am so glad to be an AR. I feel it gives me an advantage on adapting, and not knowing answers and being ok with searching for answers.

Downside to being an AR: here is the list from the master:

  1. Having to explain or justify feelings
  2. Competition
  3. Working with dictatorial/authoritarian personalities
  4. Working in a restrictive environment
  5. Working with people who don't seem friendly
  6. Concentrating on one thing at a time
  7. Giving exact details
  8. Accepting even positive criticism


Wow that is a lot! The biggest ones for me are #1, 3, 5. So I will need to make sure I assert myself in a healthy way in group situations, and also stay on task instead of getting caught on a research rabbit trail. And I also need to avoid control freaks (or assert myself when with them).

The big thing, I think, is working from a growth mindset perspective. A fixed mindset is death to learning. In the coding world it is impossible to know everything or keep up with the lateste innovations; the profession requires being open to learning and open to the reality that I do not know everything. It is comforting to know that I am not expected to know everything. But I am expected to learn fast and be able to find what I need to learn. Those expectations fit me nicely, thank God.

So far, no meltdowns. Let's see what week 4 brings.
